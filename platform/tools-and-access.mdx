## Why tool governance matters

Most agent risk comes from tool access, not text generation. Xenovia keeps tool permissions close to runtime policy decisions.

## Access model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Discover">Runtime traffic registers agent/tool identities under a proxy.</Step>
  <Step title="Classify">Annotate owner, environment, and criticality.</Step>
  <Step title="Scope">Define allowed operations per proxy and runtime identity.</Step>
  <Step title="Enforce">Run checks before every tool call.</Step>
  <Step title="Record">Persist allowed, denied, and escalated outcomes.</Step>
</Steps>

## Recommended controls

- Least-privilege credentials per proxy and environment.
- Separate permissions by environment (`dev`, `staging`, `prod`).
- Approval requirement for destructive operations.
- Periodic review of unused or high-risk grants.

## Evidence requirements

Each tool interaction should retain:

- Proxy identity + runtime-derived caller identity
- Requested operation
- Scope and policy comparison result
- Final decision, timestamp, and operator involvement (if escalated)
